The data is not ambiguous: when the largest entities are net buyers while retail is net sellers, it has been one of the highest-confidence long-term entry signals in crypto every single time it has occurred.

3-Month ETF Outflow Streak — First Since Inception
January marked the third consecutive month of net outflows from US spot Bitcoin ETFs — the first such streak since their launch in January 2024. Total AUM nearly halved from ~$110B peak to $84.3B. The 5-week stretch alone erased ~$4B — the longest single outflow run since February 2025. Institutional 13F filings confirm ~25,000 BTC in net selling during Q4 2025.

Geographic Divergence — US Alone, Not Global
While the US ETF complex saw $403M in outflows, Germany, Canada, and Switzerland showed significant combined inflows. This geographic split suggests the selling is driven by US-specific macro fear (tariff shock, recession risk) rather than a structural rejection of Bitcoin. Non-US institutional capital is still buying.

Analysts at CoinDesk distinguish between "sticky" long-term institutional capital and "tactical" capital. MicroStrategy (Strategy), Mubadala, Harvard endowment: not selling.
