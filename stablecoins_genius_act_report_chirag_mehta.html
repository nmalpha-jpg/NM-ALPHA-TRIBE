01Regulation & Macro Context

The GENIUS Act: How One Law Changed Everything for Stablecoins

Signed into law in July 2025, the Guiding and Establishing National Innovation for U.S. Stablecoins Act created the first comprehensive federal regulatory framework for payment stablecoins in US history. The market's response was immediate and unmistakable: stablecoin supply grew 7% in July–August alone — the fastest single two-month surge ever recorded.

The GENIUS Act isn't just a regulatory milestone — it's a structural demand accelerant. The law mandates 1:1 reserve backing with high-quality liquid assets, monthly public attestations, annual independent audits, and full Bank Secrecy Act / AML compliance. What this effectively does is create two classes of stablecoin: GENIUS-compliant instruments (backed primarily by US Treasuries and cash equivalents), and everything else. Institutional capital is, predictably, flowing toward the former.

The Regulatory Timeline That Got Us Here

MAY 2025 · US SENATE
GENIUS Act Introduced
Senator Bill Haggerty introduces the bill. Clears the Senate in June on bipartisan support — a rarity in US crypto legislation.
JULY 2025 · SIGNED INTO LAW
President Trump Signs GENIUS Act
First federal stablecoin framework in US history. Stablecoin supply grows 7% in July–August alone — fastest two-month surge ever recorded.
DEC 2025 · OCC
Banking Charters Approved
Five national trust bank charters provisionally approved: Circle, Ripple, Paxos, BitGo, and Fidelity Digital Assets — giving them access to Federal Reserve master accounts.
DEC 2025 · FDIC
First Stablecoin Rulemaking
FDIC approves its first proposed rulemaking for bank-issued stablecoins. JPMorgan, BofA, Citi, Wells Fargo begin joint stablecoin consortium talks.
FEB 2026 · CHINA
PBoC Defines RWA / Stablecoins
People's Bank of China formally classifies unauthorized stablecoin tokenization as illegal — effectively exiting the market and ceding global stablecoin influence to US-regulated issuers.
Q1–Q2 2026 · EXPECTED
GENIUS Act Implementation Kicks In
Full enforcement expected as early as late March 2026. Non-compliant issuers face deadline pressure. Capital rotation toward GENIUS-compliant stablecoins accelerates.
💡 The Big Picture
Stablecoins have decisively graduated from a crypto-native trading tool into a foundational layer of global financial infrastructure. The numbers prove it: stablecoin transaction volumes in 2025 exceeded those of Visa and Mastercard combined on an annualised basis. Natalia Karayaneva, CEO of Propy, captured it best: "Stablecoins stopped being 'crypto products' and started acting like infrastructure." The GENIUS Act didn't create this shift — it ratified it and accelerated it.

USDT: The $187B Giant With a Crack in Its Foundation

Tether's USDT remains the undisputed king of stablecoins — $187B in circulation, 60%+ market dominance, and the backbone of global crypto liquidity. But S&P's November 2025 downgrade to "Weak" (the lowest possible score) flagged a structural vulnerability that no issuer of this scale has ever faced. Is USDT too big to fail, or too big to fix?

Why the S&P downgrade matters more than Tether admits: Tether's BTC holdings represent approximately 5.6% of USDT in circulation — and that exceeds Tether's 3.9% overcollateralization buffer. In plain English: if Bitcoin drops significantly, Tether's reserves cannot fully absorb the decline, and USDT could theoretically become undercollateralized. A $19.7B drop in BTC value following a 20% correction would eliminate Tether's entire buffer.
What makes this uniquely dangerous is that USDT doesn't have full audit coverage — only quarterly attestations from BDO Italy, not Big Four audits. S&P specifically flagged "limited transparency on reserve management and risk appetite" and "no asset segregation to protect against issuer insolvency." These aren't new criticisms. But the GENIUS Act, when fully implemented, may force Tether to choose: restructure reserves to qualify as compliant, or cede regulated markets entirely.
"We wear your loathing with pride."
Paolo Ardoino, Tether CEO — responding to S&P's 'Weak' downgrade, Nov 2025
⚠️
The Core Risk: GENIUS Act Compliance
The GENIUS Act requires 100% liquid reserve backing (short-term gov bonds, money market funds, repo). Tether's current reserve mix — with BTC, gold, secured loans, and limited audit transparency — may not qualify. If Tether cannot or will not comply, it risks being excluded from US-regulated markets. USDT delisting already happened on EU exchanges under MiCA.
HIGH RISK FLAG
🔍 The Tether Paradox
Tether is simultaneously the most dominant and most structurally vulnerable actor in the stablecoin space. It controls 60%+ of the market. It earns $10B+ annually from Treasury yields on its massive float — making it one of the most profitable financial entities in the world relative to headcount. And yet it has never undergone a full Big Four audit, carries BTC in reserves that exceeds its overcollateralization buffer, and has already been delisted from EU exchanges for MiCA non-compliance. HSBC analysts put it precisely: "Institutional and corporate adoption will likely reward the best-regulated, highest-quality reserves" — which structurally redirects new institutional flows away from USDT regardless of its dominance.

USDC: The Compliance Bet That's Paying Off

Circle's USDC grew from $53B to $75B in 2025 — a 78% YoY surge — while leading all stablecoins by transaction flow at $18.3T. Then came the IPO, the banking charter, and the Visa integration. USDC isn't just winning the regulatory race. It's becoming the default dollar for the institutional internet.

Three events in 2025 structurally elevated USDC's position: First, Circle completed its IPO (trading as CRCL), giving it public market accountability and the balance sheet transparency that institutional allocators demand. Second, the OCC provisionally approved Circle's national trust bank charter — giving it access to Federal Reserve infrastructure. Third, Visa launched USDC settlement on Solana in December 2025, with Cross River Bank and Lead Bank as the first banking participants — and broader US rollout planned through 2026.
The reserve picture is the starkest contrast with USDT. USDC is backed entirely by cash and short-dated US Treasury securities, with monthly independent audits (not just quarterly attestations). Every reserve dollar is documented. Every custodian is disclosed. This isn't just a regulatory advantage — it's the exact structure the GENIUS Act was written to enable.
🏦
Circle Arc: L1 Built for USDC
Circle is developing Arc, a purpose-built Layer 1 blockchain for USDC-native activity. Visa is already a design partner and plans to operate a validator node. This isn't just a product — it's infrastructure that would make USDC the settlement primitive of a new payments chain.
BULLISH LONG-TERM
⚡
The 2023 SVB Moment: A Cautionary Note
In March 2023, USDC briefly de-pegged to $0.87 after Circle disclosed $3.3B in reserves were held at Silicon Valley Bank during its collapse. A centralised stablecoin's peg is only as strong as its banking relationships — a lesson the market has not forgotten.

The New Entrants: Banks, Fintechs, and the Synthetic Challengers Reshaping Payments

2025 saw the number of stablecoins above $10M in supply double from 75 to 142. Stripe acquired Bridge for over $1B. Visa launched USDC settlement. JPMorgan, BofA, Citi, and Wells Fargo formed a joint stablecoin consortium. PayPal expanded PYUSD. And Ethena's USDe hit $14B in supply. The stablecoin market is no longer a two-horse race.

The Three Most Consequential Entrants

💸
Stripe + Bridge
Stripe acquired Bridge — the stablecoin API infrastructure company — for over $1B in 2025. Bridge won the bid to issue USDH on Hyperliquid. Stripe is embedding stablecoin functionality as a developer primitive: payouts, wallet balances, corporate card settlement. With $1.9T in 2025 payment volume, even backend stablecoin adoption at the margin moves material volume. The pitch: access stablecoins as easily as card or bank rails.
DISTRIBUTION MOAT
📊
Ethena USDe
The synthetic dollar. USDe hit $14B in 2025 — the fastest growth of any new stablecoin in history. It maintains its peg through delta-hedging: staked ETH as collateral + short perpetual positions. The GENIUS Act's prohibition on yield-bearing stablecoins for US retail paradoxically drove a 70% supply surge since July 2025, as non-US users sought yield. Risk: the mechanism unwinds violently in extreme market dislocations. Not GENIUS-compliant — positioned as DeFi infrastructure, not payments.
HIGH RISK / HIGH YIELD
🏛️
JPM / Big Bank Consortium
JPMorgan Chase, BofA, Citigroup, and Wells Fargo began joint stablecoin consortium discussions in May 2025. Their product: a fully collateralised digital token, redeemable through member banks, extending the dollar into programmable form. Citigroup CEO Jane Fraser confirmed the bank is actively considering issuing its own stablecoin. The irony: these institutions spent years warning about crypto risk. By mid-2025, they were designing their own digital dollar. Fintech pressure left them no choice.
WATCH: H2 2026 LAUNCH
💡 The Infrastructure Shift in One Paragraph
Visa launched USDC settlement on Solana in December 2025 — the first time a global card network settled transactions on a public blockchain. Monthly crypto card spend grew from $100M in early 2023 to $1.5B by late 2025, implying $18B annualised. Stripe processed $1.9 trillion in payment volume in 2025 and is now embedding stablecoin APIs for merchant payouts. PayPal is expanding PYUSD to 100+ countries. Stablecoin settlement is not approaching mainstream payments infrastructure. It is already inside it.

2026 Outlook: The Five Dynamics That Will Define the Next 12 Months

The stablecoin market has crossed $307B and processed $33T in 2025 transactions. Regulation is coming into force. Banks are building. Fintechs have a head start. Here's the framework for thinking about where value accrues — and what can go wrong — over the next 12 months.

5 Dynamics to Watch in 2026

📋
1. GENIUS Act Full Enforcement (Q1–Q2 2026)
Full implementation expected as early as late March 2026. Non-compliant issuers (read: Tether in its current form) face hard choices. Capital rotation toward GENIUS-compliant instruments — USDC, Paxos, bank-issued tokens — could be the single largest structural flow of the year.
ACCELERANT FOR USDC
🏦
2. Big Bank Stablecoin Launch
JPMorgan, Citigroup, BofA, and Wells Fargo have all signalled intent. A joint bank consortium stablecoin would instantly command institutional trust, deep liquidity, and Federal Reserve infrastructure access. If launched in H2 2026, it resets the competitive map entirely — and validates the asset class at the highest possible level.
MARKET LEGITIMISING
🌍
3. Emerging Market Dollar Substitution
Standard Chartered projects $1 trillion in bank deposits from emerging markets could flow into stablecoins. In countries where the local currency is weak — Argentina, Nigeria, Turkey — USDT is already functioning as a shadow dollar banking system. This use case is structurally durable and largely immune to US regulatory risk.
LONG-DURATION GROWTH
⚡
4. Non-USD Stablecoins Proliferate
Qivalis (10 European banks, Euro-pegged, H2 2026 launch), Sony Bank (yen-denominated), and a growing slate of EUR/GBP stablecoins will launch under MiCA. The shift: companies and consumers in local markets will no longer need to convert through USD stablecoins for on-chain activity. A multi-currency programmable money layer emerges.
WATCH: EURO STABLECOIN LAUNCH
⚠️
5. Tether's Reserve Crisis Risk
If BTC drops 30%+, Tether's overcollateralization buffer is eliminated. A simultaneous redemption wave — even at 5% of circulating supply — would require $9B+ in liquid assets. Tether has survived this scenario before. But in a GENIUS Act world, with institutional capital now preferring compliant instruments, redemption pressure could be qualitatively different from prior cycles.
TAIL RISK: HIGH IMPACT

📊 My 2026 Framework: Where Value Accrues
The stablecoin market is bifurcating along regulatory lines. GENIUS-compliant instruments (USDC, Paxos, bank-issued tokens) will capture the majority of new institutional and payments flows. Tether will retain dominance in emerging markets and DeFi, where GENIUS compliance is irrelevant — but will increasingly cede regulated institutional markets. Ethena's USDe serves a specific DeFi-native yield niche that grows or collapses with DeFi TVL. The biggest 2026 opportunity is not picking which stablecoin wins — it's identifying which infrastructure layer wins: Stripe's API stack, Circle's Arc blockchain, Visa's settlement rails, or the bank consortium's tokenised deposit system. The stablecoin itself is the commodity. The rails around it are where the durable moat lives.
